Stine Maiken Brix, born April 3rd 1982 at Glostrup Hospital.


Member period
Member of Parliament for The Red-Green Alliance in North Jutland greater constituency from September 15th 2011.
Candidate for The Red-Green Alliance in Aalborg East nomination district from 2010.
Candidate for The Red-Green Alliance in Aabenraa nomination district from 2009 to 2010.

Parliamentary career
Member of the Presidium from July 3rd 2015 to January 31st 2016.

Spokeswoman on health, prevention and psychiatry affairs. Chairwoman of the parliamentary group of the Red-Green Alliance from 2013.


Education and jobs
Reading medicine, University of Copenhagen, from 2006.
Basic course in social studies, Roskilde University, from 2003 to 2005.
Matriculation certificate, Albertslund Upper Secondary School, from 1998 to 2001.
Primary school, Teglmose School, Albertslund, from 1988 to 1998.

Campaign and communications employee, the Red-Green Alliance, from 2010 to 2011.
Journalist, 'Universitetsavisen', from 2007 to 2010.
Temporary nurse, Bispebjerg Acute Admission Ward, FADL's temp agency, from 2007 to 2008.
Nursing home assistant, Engskrænten Nursing Home, Rødovre, from 2002 to 2005.


Affiliations
Member of the central board and executive committee of the Union of Danish Upper Secondary School Students 2001-2002. Member of the management committee of 'Genstart Danmark' 2006. Spokeswoman for 'Kirkeasyl' 2009. Member of the central board of the Red-Green Alliance from 2009, and of the executive committee from 2014.


Publications
Author of the article »Mobbet gennem hele 1.g« (Bullied Throughout the First Year of Upper Secondary School) in the anthology »Rivaler og Syndebukke« (Rivals and Scapegoats), Systime, 2001, of the article »Velfærdskampen« (The Welfare Struggle) in the anthology »Brudflader - Økonomi og samfund 2008« (Fractured Surfaces – Economics and Society), Frydenlund, 2007, and of the article »Sejre og nederlag« (Victories and Defeats) in the anthology »Kirkeasyl - en kamp for ophold«, (Church Asylum – A Struggle to Stay), Frydenlund, 2011.
